## Overview

A hands-on workshop designed to help both developers and non-developers build a working MVP (Minimum Viable Product) in under 3 hours using AI-powered development tools. Attendees brought their laptops, picked a problem to solve, and shipped working prototypes by the end of the session.

## Format

- **Type**: Hands-on workshop
- **Venue**: CODE, Ankara
- **Duration**: ~3 hours
- **Speakers**: 2
- **Structure**: Brief intro + guided building + peer support + demos
- **Audience**: Developers and non-developers welcome

## Highlights

- Real-time MVP building with AI tools like Cursor and Replit
- Participants chose their own problems to solve
- Mix of technical and non-technical attendees collaborating
- Focus on shipping something functional, not perfect
- Peer learning and support throughout the session

## Key Takeaways

- AI tools dramatically reduce the barrier to building functional prototypes
- Non-developers can ship working products with the right guidance
- Constraints (3-hour limit) force focus on core value proposition
- Community support accelerates learning and problem-solving
- "Done is better than perfect" mindset is essential for MVPs

## Topics Covered

- Setting up development environment with AI tools
- Prompt engineering for code generation
- Rapid prototyping workflows
- Debugging with AI assistance
- Deploying MVPs quickly
